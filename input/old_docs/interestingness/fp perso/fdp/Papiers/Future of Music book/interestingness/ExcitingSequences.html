Exciting sequences

Temporal sequences abound in our world:  sequences of words in a sentence, of
sentences in a book, of shots in a movie, of chords in a piano comping,
of notes in a melody, of music titles on the radio.
Sequences of games in a tennis match.
Of events in the day, in life.

What is it that makes some of these temporal sequences exciting or boring ?
And what makes them exciting or boring for me and not for others?

To understand the nature of exciting and boring sequences,
I propose a game.
The game consists in producing sequences, and trying to make them as exciting
as possible.
To make the game simple, let us consider very simple sequences:
sequences of bits: 1 and 0.
Things like:
00000000000000000000000000000000000000000000000
or
10000000000000000010101010101010101010101001001
or even
00100100001001000000000000000000000000000000000

There are two players: a producer and a listener.
The producer produces the sequence item by item (that is here, bit by bit).
At each step, the listener may either:

  • Do nothing. He waits for the next items.
  • Declare he is bored (button B)
  • Declare he is excited (button E)
  • Stop the game, it is so boring. (button S)
The challenge is to retain the attention of the listener, i.e. to produce a sequence
such that the listener will stop listening as late as possible.

Let's play.
